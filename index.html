PCD2026 — Poetics of Computational Design PCD2026 marks the sixth edition of an event that originated within the international Processing Community Day network an open-source, community-driven initiative rooted in shared learning, experimentation, and creative coding practices. Over the years, PCD@Porto has grown and expanded. What began as an event primarily centred on Processing and p5.js gradually expanded into a broader space for artistic, design-oriented, and critical explorations of computation. In 2026, we choose to preserve the acronym PCD, strongly associated with the event’s identity and community, while redefining its meaning. PCD now stands for Poetics of Computational Design. This shift does not represent a departure from our origins, but rather their natural development. We continue to share the foundational values of the Processing Community Day openness, inclusivity, knowledge sharing, and a critical spirit, while broadening the scope to embrace diverse tools, methods, and computational practices.
2026 theme Reflect · Resist · Reform In a time marked by technological acceleration, automation, and the increasing normalization of systems that shape behaviour, perception, and thought, PCD2026 proposes a moment of pause and reconsideration. “As he saw it, people will come to love their oppression, to adore the technologies that undo their capacities to think.” — Neil Postman, Amusing Ourselves to Death
